About This Tool

This tool was inspired by this perspective on cycling performance:

1/
Cyclists get one power-duration curve. Best 5s, best 1min, best 20min, best hour: the maximum effort at each duration, traced into a single line. That curve is the standard object for profiling, modeling, and comparing riders.

— one of you (@Captain7bag) June 16, 2026

While the standard Power Duration curve shows your absolute best, it often fails to represent what you can consistently do on a typical day. I built this tool because I needed a way to see not just my peak performance, but what is realistic during a Sunday ride with friends—helping you understand the pace you can maintain on a 15-minute climb without blowing up.

Data Quality & Coverage

The accuracy and depth of these charts depend entirely on the data you provide. To get a realistic view of your "repeatable" power, you should include a wide range of activities: your strongest rides, race efforts, and even your "bad days." Including only your best rides will skew the percentiles upward and won't reflect your actual day-to-day capability.
